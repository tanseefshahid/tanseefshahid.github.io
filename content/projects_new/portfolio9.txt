Real-Time Anomaly Detection & Multi-Camera Tracking System (Incheon Airport)
Project Overview
Architected and developed a real-time video surveillance pipeline for Incheon International Airport research to identify anomalous human behaviors and track flagged individuals across a distributed camera network. The system enhanced security response times by automating the detection of critical events in highly crowded environments.

The Challenge
Airport security environments present extreme challenges due to severe occlusions, dynamic lighting, and dense crowds. The objective was to accurately detect rare, high-stakes anomalies—such as severe falls, sudden sprinting, rushing through immigration checkpoints, and abandoned baggage—and seamlessly track the identified subject across multiple non-overlapping camera feeds.

Technical Approach & Architecture

Hybrid Action Recognition Pipeline:

Deployed YOWO (You Only Watch Once) as a base 3D-CNN spatiotemporal feature extractor to detect high-level action bounding boxes in real-time video streams.

Integrated a Human Pose Estimation model (e.g., HRNet/OpenPose) to extract skeletal kinematics. Leveraged this geometric data to filter false positives generated by YOWO. For example, severe falls were validated by calculating the rapid velocity and spatial orientation changes of specific keypoints (e.g., shoulders and hips) relative to the ground plane.

Complex Event & Interaction Logic:

Engineered custom spatio-temporal heuristics to detect "abandoned baggage" by monitoring Human-Object Interaction (HOI) over time, flagging when a tracked person and a stationary object bounding box permanently diverged.

Implemented trajectory and speed analysis to detect "dashing" or unauthorized rushing through secure immigration zones.

Multi-Target Multi-Camera Tracking (MTMCT):

Designed a multi-camera tracking pipeline combining DeepSORT for temporal tracking within a single frame and a robust Person Re-Identification (Re-ID) embedding model.

This allowed the system to extract appearance features of a flagged individual and maintain their unique ID as they moved across different, disconnected camera fields of view (FOV).

Impact & Results

Enhanced Threat Response: Delivered a real-time alert system capable of analyzing high-resolution CCTV feeds, significantly reducing the cognitive load on security personnel by filtering out normal crowd behavior.

Robust Tracking: Successfully maintained the identities of flagged individuals across the airport concourse, demonstrating the viability of automated MTMCT in heavily occluded public spaces.

Tools & Technologies

Computer Vision: YOWO (Spatiotemporal Action Detection), HRNet / OpenPose (Kinematics), Person Re-ID

Tracking Algorithms: DeepSORT, MTMCT techniques

Languages & Frameworks: Python, PyTorch, OpenCV
